⚠️ Warning Signs You Need Waterproofing

  • Water stains on basement walls or ceiling
  • Musty or moldy smell in lower levels
  • Efflorescence (white mineral deposits) on walls
  • Cracks in foundation walls
  • Peeling paint or bubbling on interior walls
  • Standing water after rain in basement or crawl space
  • Damp or wet spots on floors
  • Higher humidity levels in your building

If you notice any of these signs, get a professional inspection before the damage spreads.

Why Queens & Brooklyn Buildings Need Waterproofing

Aging Building Stock

Many Queens and Brooklyn buildings are 50-100+ years old. Original waterproofing materials have long since degraded. Without modern waterproofing systems, these buildings are vulnerable to water damage that accelerates structural deterioration.

High Water Table

Parts of Queens and Brooklyn — especially near the coast and former marshland — have high water tables that put constant hydrostatic pressure on foundation walls. Proper drainage and waterproofing is essential in these areas.

NYC Weather Extremes

Heavy rain events are increasing in frequency. NYC gets 50+ inches of rain per year, plus snow and ice. Buildings without proper waterproofing face repeated water intrusion that causes cumulative damage over time.

Building Code Compliance

NYC building codes require waterproofing for new construction and major renovations. If you're renovating, adding a roof deck, or converting space, waterproofing may be required to pass inspection.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does waterproofing cost in NYC?

Costs vary significantly based on the scope. Interior basement waterproofing starts at $2,500-$5,000. Exterior foundation waterproofing runs $5,000-$15,000 depending on building size. Roof waterproofing ranges from $1,500 for coatings to $8,000+ for full membrane systems. We provide detailed free estimates.

Is interior or exterior waterproofing better?

Exterior waterproofing stops water before it reaches your walls — it's the most effective but more expensive (requires excavation). Interior waterproofing manages water that's already entered — it's less disruptive and more affordable. Often, a combination approach works best. We'll recommend the right solution for your specific situation.

How long does waterproofing last?

Quality waterproofing systems last 15-25 years or more. Exterior membrane systems can last the life of the building. Interior solutions like sump pumps need periodic maintenance. We use premium materials and provide warranties on all our work.

Can you waterproof during winter?

Interior waterproofing can be done year-round. Exterior work is best done in warmer months when the ground isn't frozen, though emergency repairs are always available. Roof waterproofing with torch-applied systems can be done in cold weather.

Do I need waterproofing if I'm building a roof deck?

Yes — it's required by code and essential for protecting the building below. Before any roof deck is installed, a proper waterproof membrane system must be in place. We install the waterproofing layer that goes beneath your deck structure.
