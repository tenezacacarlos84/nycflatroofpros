What Causes Emergency Roof Leaks?

  • Storm Damage — Heavy rain, wind, and hail can puncture or tear flat roof membranes
  • Clogged Drains — Blocked roof drains cause water to pond and find weak spots
  • Failed Flashing — Old flashing around walls, pipes, and edges lets water in during heavy rain
  • Membrane Deterioration — Aging roof membranes crack and split, especially after temperature extremes
  • Construction Damage — HVAC work, satellite dish installation, or foot traffic punctures the membrane
  • Ice Dams — Winter freeze-thaw cycles force water under flashing and through cracks

What To Do While You Wait

  1. Contain the water — Place buckets under drips, use towels to dam spreading water
  2. Move valuables — Get furniture, electronics, and important items away from the leak
  3. Document everything — Take photos/video of the leak and damage for insurance
  4. Don't go on the roof — Wet flat roofs are extremely slippery and dangerous
  5. Turn off electricity — If water is near electrical fixtures, turn off power in that area
